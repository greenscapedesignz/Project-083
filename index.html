Why This Project Matters

The Problems We Face Together

Every monsoon, every school run, every morning walk — these are the failures our neighbourhood experiences. Project 083 addresses each of them, together.

🚗
Streets Captured by Derelict Cars
Commercial garages dump abandoned vehicles on public right-of-way — blocking footpaths, choking stormwater drains, and turning shared streets into private junkyards. The park edge and neighbourhood roads alike are affected.
🌊
64 Crore Litres of Rain Lost Every Monsoon
The rajakaluve is concretised and disconnected from the natural catchment. Every year, 640 million litres of rainwater floods our streets, businesses, and homes instead of recharging groundwater and replenishing the lake — because the system is broken.
🐟
The Tavarekere Lake Is Dying
Raw sewage flows directly into the lake. Concrete walls prevent natural bank exchange. Aquatic biodiversity has collapsed. The water body that historically supported the neighbourhood ecosystem now threatens public health.
🚶
No Safe Space to Walk, Cycle, or Rest
Footpaths are occupied by vehicles, broken, or non-existent. There is no cycling infrastructure. Elderly residents and children have no safe, shaded public space. The park's high walls ensure it benefits no one passing by.
🗑️
Solid Waste Has No System
Chronic dumping hotspots line the rajakaluve banks and lake edge. Without structured waste segregation and collection, every intervention is undermined — bioswales clog, drains block, and the public realm degrades within months of any improvement.
🌿
Exotic Plants Replace Native Ecology
BBMP horticulture routinely plants invasive and ecologically inappropriate exotic species — Gulmohar, Rain Tree, Bougainvillea, Lantana — in parks and medians, displacing native flora and reducing the corridor's capacity to support biodiversity and aquifer recharge.
The Transformation

What We Are Building

Project 083 transforms 850 metres of degraded Tavarekere park edge — from Bhabani Nagar in the north to 1st Main Tank Bund Road in the south — into a continuous, shaded, ecology-first public corridor with safe walkways, a dedicated cycling track, skating zones, spaces for elderly citizens and children, a rejuvenated rajakaluve that recharges our groundwater, a living lake with bioengineered edges and floating wetlands, and city-wide policies that prevent this from ever happening again.
